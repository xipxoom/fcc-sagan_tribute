Life Summary

Dr. Carl Sagan (1934-1996) was a popular author, astrophysicist, astronomer, researcher, and science communicator who published more than 600 papers and books throughout his life. Dr. Sagan won the Pulitzer Prize for his book The Dragons of Eden while his book Cosmos became the best selling science book ever published.

NASA awarded Dr. Sagan the Apollo Achievement Award as well as the twice awarding him the Medals for Exceptional Scientific Achievement and Distinguished Public Service. He was a founder of The Planetary Society, which to this day collaborates with NASA and space programs around the world on missions and research.

To learn more about Dr. Sagan's life and broad span of accomplishments, visit his Wikipedia page.
